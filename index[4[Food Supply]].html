Access to Food Supply


Various government agencies, non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and international partners are collaborating to establish and maintain food and water systems for displaced populations. Refugee camps and reception centers have designated dining areas and distribution centers where food and drinking water are provided on a regular basis. These provisions comply with established nutritional guidelines and safety standards, taking into account the dietary preferences, cultural background and any special dietary needs or restrictions of refugees. In addition, efforts are being made to ensure the availability and accessibility of food and water for refugees living outside formal camps, whether in urban areas or in temporary accommodation. By prioritizing the provision of sufficient and nutritious food and safe drinking water, stakeholders seek to protect the health, dignity and resilience of refugees amid challenges of displacement and uncertainty.






National food and "Tavernas"


Greek cuisine is known throughout the world for its emphasis on fresh, seasonal ingredients, simple yet delicious dishes and a rich palette of regional dishes that reflect the country's cultural heritage. Traditional Greek dishes such as moussaka, souvlaki, spanakopita and tzatziki, which usually have olive oil, herbs, vegetables and grains as the main ingredients.

Taverns, or "tavernas" in Greek, serve as quintessential meeting places where locals and visitors come together to enjoy the atmosphere and delicious cuisine that define the Greek gastronomic experience. These establishments range from family-run tavernas located in picturesque villages to bustling seaside taverns overlooking the waters of the Aegean Sea. At the taverns, diners can enjoy a wide selection of meze (snacks), including freshly baked bread, olives, feta cheese and tzatziki, before moving on to heartier main courses such as grilled meats, seafood and other dishes.
