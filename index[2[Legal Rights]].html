Thessaloniki:

Legal Rights

Refugees in Thessaloniki are granted special legal rights under Greek and international law. Refugees retain the right to apply for asylum and have the right to a decision on their refugee status. The Greek Asylum Service takes responsibility for processing asylum applications and providing legal assistance throughout the asylum decision process.

Refugees also have the right to access health care, education and basic social services. In Greece, numerous government and non-government organizations offer legal assistance and protection to refugees, helping them navigate the asylum process and protect their rights.

However, Greece faces enormous challenges in managing the influx of refugees, and human rights groups have raised concerns about conditions in refugee camps and the treatment of asylum seekers. Despite these challenges, Greece remains bound by international legal obligations to protect the rights of refugees and ensure that they are treated with dignity and respect.

1. Asylum procedures:


Refugees in Thessaloniki and Greece are given the legal right to seek asylum, a fundamental process governed by strict guidelines. Traveling to seek asylum entails formally submitting an application to either the Greek Asylum Service or the Greek Asylum Appeals Committee, which are the key bodies responsible for the comprehensive assessment of each case. Once an application is submitted, a thorough assessment is carried out to determine the validity of the refugee claim. This process is regulated by international legal norms and domestic legislation. Throughout the process, refugees have the right to legal representation and assistance, ensuring that their rights are respected and their cases are effectively pursued. It should be noted that the asylum process is multifaceted and may involve various steps, including interviews, documentation reviews and appeals processes, all of which require close attention to detail and adherence to procedures. Ultimately, the determination of refugee status profoundly impacts an individual's legal status and access to fundamental rights and protections in the host country, highlighting the critical importance of this initial procedural step.

2. Access to legal assistance (advocate,lawyer):


In the legal sphere of Thessaloniki and Greece, refugees have an inalienable right to seek and receive legal aid, a vital resource needed to navigate the asylum system. Recognizing the complexities and challenges inherent in the asylum process, there are numerous legal aid organizations and advocacy groups in the region offering free or low-cost legal services to refugees. These organizations play a key role in providing refugees with critical guidance, representation and protection at various stages of the asylum process, ensuring that their rights are respected and their voices are heard. Moreover, legal aid empowers refugees by providing them with the necessary knowledge and resources to meaningfully participate in legal proceedings. Together, these efforts strengthen the integrity of the asylum system and contribute to the realization of justice for refugees seeking protection and asylum in Greece.

3. Protection from deportation:


Central to the legal framework governing refugees in Thessaloniki and Greece is the principle of non-refoulement, a cornerstone of international refugee law, which prohibits the forced return of people to areas where they would face persecution or serious harm. As a signatory to various international treaties and conventions, including the 1951 Refugee Convention and the European Convention on Human Rights, Greece is obliged to uphold and protect this fundamental principle by ensuring that refugees under its jurisdiction are provided with adequate protection and asylum. The ban on refoulement highlights the need for a thorough and impartial assessment of asylum claims, thereby reducing the risk that people will be subject to serious human rights violations or persecution. Moreover, this principle serves as a safeguard against arbitrary or unlawful deportations by reaffirming the host country's commitment to maintaining the dignity, safety and well-being of refugees within its borders. As such, protection from refoulement represents a fundamental defense against the perpetuation of injustice and persecution, embodying the core principles of compassion, solidarity and respect for human rights in refugee protection.

4. Freedom of movement:


Within the legal framework of Thessaloniki and Greece, refugees enjoy the fundamental right to freedom of movement, a cornerstone of individual freedom and autonomy enshrined in both national and international law. This fundamental right gives refugees autonomy to move within the host country, facilitating their integration, access to basic services and pursuit of livelihood opportunities. However, it is important to recognize that certain restrictions and limitations on movement may be imposed, particularly where people are housed in reception centers or camps. These restrictions are often used as a measure to manage and regulate population movements, maintain security, and ensure the effective delivery of services to refugees living in these facilities. While restrictions on movement may pose challenges, efforts are being made to balance the need to protect individual rights with the need to address logistical and administrative issues in the context of managing and supporting refugees.

5. Access to services:


A critical component of refugee protection in Thessaloniki and Greece is ensuring equal access to essential services, such as health care, education, social assistance and other vital resources necessary for the well-being and integration of refugees into the host society. Recognizing the diverse needs and vulnerabilities of refugees, government agencies, international organizations and civil society actors are making concerted efforts to establish and maintain robust service delivery mechanisms tailored to the unique circumstances and challenges faced by displaced populations. These services are designed to mitigate the impact of displacement, meet humanitarian needs and promote the social inclusion and empowerment of refugees, thereby facilitating their transition to self-reliance and resilience. Moreover, the provision of essential services not only fulfills the host country's obligations under international law, but also reflects its commitment to upholding the principles of humanity, solidarity and respect for human dignity in the treatment of refugees. By ensuring equal access to services, Thessaloniki and Greece affirm their role as responsible stakeholders in global efforts to provide protection and assistance to displaced populations fleeing persecution, conflict and deprivation.

6. Work and Social rights:


Fundamental to the legal framework governing the situation of refugees in Thessaloniki and Greece is the recognition of their inalienable right to work and access to social rights on an equal basis with citizens, thereby facilitating their integration and self-sufficiency in the host society. Refugees granted asylum or subsidiary protection have the right to seek paid employment and enjoy social rights, including access to health care, education, housing and social assistance, commensurate with the rights granted to citizens. However, there may be practical problems in realizing these rights, including barriers related to language skills, recognition of qualifications and discriminatory labor market practices. Thus, efforts are being made to remove these barriers through targeted interventions, including language and vocational training programs, employment opportunities initiatives, and advocacy for the recognition of refugee skills and qualifications. By supporting refugees' rights to work and access to social protection, Thessaloniki and Greece reaffirm their commitment to promoting inclusion, dignity and economic independence among displaced populations, thereby promoting their long-term integration and well-being in the host society.
