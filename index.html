Ancient Heritage

Ancient Greece was a center of intellectual, artistic and philosophical achievement. This cradle of Western civilization gave birth to some of humanity's most enduring achievements: the birth of democracy in Athens, the philosophical reflections of Socrates, Plato and Aristotle. Mythology, rich in tales of gods and heroes, captivates the imagination, shaping literature, art and culture. The architecture of the Acropolis, as well as the Parthenon, is a testament to the mastery of ancient Greek civilization. From the Olympic Games to theatrical drama, the influence of Ancient Greece resonates across all disciplines, highlighting its profound influence on the development of human thought and expression.

Climate in Greece

The climate of Greece is Mediterranean in nature: hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. During the summer months, from June to August, temperatures rise sharply, often reaching a maximum of 35-40 degrees. Coastal areas benefit from refreshing sea breezes, providing some relief from the heat. In contrast, winter, from December to February, brings cooler temperatures, especially in northern regions and mountainous areas where snowfall is common. Rainfall is more common during the winter months, creating the landscapes that characterize the Greek countryside. In general, the Mediterranean climate favors a lifestyle focused on leisure and leisurely living in the fresh air, with ample opportunities for walking and dining in the fresh air of Greece almost all year round.

Accommodation Options

For people considering residency in Greece, the country offers a range of options tailored to different circumstances. EU/EEA citizens find the process relatively streamlined, often requiring simple registration with local authorities for long-term stays. However, non-EU/EEA nationals have several options for obtaining a residence permit, including employment, investment, education or entrepreneurship. Notably, Greece's Golden Visa program has gained prominence by providing residence permits to non-EU/EEA nationals and their families who invest in real estate. This program has become an attractive opportunity for investors looking to gain a foothold in Europe while enjoying the Mediterranean lifestyle.

Healthcare System in Greece

Greece boasts a comprehensive healthcare system that provides affordable healthcare services to both residents and visitors. The country's health care system includes both the public and private sectors, with public health care financed through social security contributions and taxes. Citizens and legal residents are entitled to health insurance under the National Health System (ESY), which offers a wide range of health services, including primary care, specialist consultations, hospital care and prescription drugs. In addition, Greece has a network of public hospitals and medical facilities scattered throughout the country, providing wide access to medical services even in remote areas. Although public health services are generally of good quality, some residents choose private health care due to faster access to specialists and elective procedures, as well as higher levels of amenities and equipment.

Modern Lifestyle

In modern Greece, social life reflects a smooth integration of traditions, community ties and customs. Family gatherings on Sundays strengthen bonds over a meal of traditional dishes such as moussaka or souvlaki. The cherished value of hospitality permeates everyday interactions: neighbors exchange pleasantries and invites for a cup of coffee. Holidays such as Easter and localized commemorations bring communities together in a shared celebration marked by music and culinary delights. Coastal regions offer refuge from the summer heat, drawing locals and tourists to the pristine beaches. Evenings are characterized by leisurely dinners in taverns accompanied by ouzo or wine and bouzouki music. The city's markets are bustling with activity, facilitating lively exchanges as visitors browse the fresh produce. This intricate tapestry of modern Greek life strikes a balance between custom and the simple joys of everyday existence.

Geography

The geography of Greece is as varied as it is stunning, containing a rich variety of landscapes that will boggle the mind. Situated at the crossroads of Europe, Asia and Africa, Greece boasts a strategic location in south-eastern Europe, bordered by the Ionian, Aegean and Mediterranean seas. The country is known for its thousands of islands, from the cosmopolitan centers of Crete and Rhodes to the secluded islands of Santorini and Mykonos. Mainland Greece is characterized by mountain ranges, including the famous Mount Olympus, the mythical home of the gods. Valleys and plains grace the area, creating fertile agricultural land vital to the country's economy. To the north the country borders Albania, North Macedonia and Bulgaria, and to the east by Turkey and the Aegean Sea. The geography of Greece not only shapes but also influences its culture, history and way of life.

Navigating the Residency Process

The process of obtaining a residence permit in Greece requires attention to paperwork, documentation and specific requirements tailored to each option. While EU/EEA citizens enjoy relatively simple procedures, non-EU/EEA individuals may find the process more complex, often requiring legal advice or immigration expertise. Carefully understanding and following the rules of residence is essential, whether seeking employment, investment or educational opportunities. By seeking advice from professionals and understanding the nuances of the residency process, individuals can begin their journey to obtaining Greek residency with confidence and clarity.

Accessing Healthcare Services

Access to healthcare services in Greece is easy for residents and visitors to Greece, and a variety of options are available to suit individual needs and preferences. Citizens and legal residents can access health care through the public system by registering with the Social Security Institute (IKA) and obtaining a health card. This card provides access to primary care doctors, specialists and hospitals in the public health network. For those who prefer private healthcare, Greece boasts numerous private clinics, hospitals and medical facilities offering a wide range of services, often with shorter waiting times and more personalized care. In addition, travelers to Greece can access healthcare services through the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) or private travel insurance, providing peace of mind in the event of a medical emergency or unforeseen health problem while visiting the country.
